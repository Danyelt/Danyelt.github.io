Vision

"There is no greater disability in society, than the inabillity to see a person as more".

-Robert M. Hensei

Vision impairment is defined as a limitation of one or more functions of the eye (or visual system). The most common vision impairment affect:

  • The sharpness or clarity of vision (visiaul acuity)
  • The normal range of what you can see (visual fields)
  • Colour

The National Eye Institute defines low vision as a visual impairment not correctable by standard glasses, contact lenses, medication or surgery that interferes with the ability to perform activities of daily living. Types of visual impairment according to the CDC and the World Health Organization the classification of visual acuity and impairment includes;

  • Low visual acuity means vision between 20/70 and 20/400 with the best possible correction, or a visual field of 20 degrees or less
  • Blindness is defined as a visual acuity worse than 20/400 with the best possible correction, or a visual field of 10 degrees or less
  • Legal blindness in the US means visual acuity of 20/200 or worse with the best possible correction, or a visual field of 20 degrees or less.
  • Visual acuity of 20/70 to 20/400 (inclusive) is considered moderate visual impairment or low vision.
